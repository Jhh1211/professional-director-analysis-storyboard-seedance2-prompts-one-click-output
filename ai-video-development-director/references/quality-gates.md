# Quality Gates

Use these checks before finalizing each stage.

## Creative Diagnosis Gate

- Is the real intent identified, not just repeated?
- Is the format/genre read plausible?
- Is the dramatic or informational engine clear?
- Is there a reason the audience keeps watching?
- Are strengths and risks specific?
- Does the recommendation make a decision?

## Reference Analysis Gate

- Is the analysis about mechanics rather than surface copying?
- Are hook, structure, sound, visuals, editing, and performance all addressed?
- Is there a reusable formula?
- Is there a warning about what not to copy?

## Adaptation Gate

- Does each route actually transform the reference?
- Are the routes meaningfully different?
- Is the recommended route justified by audience, production, and story logic?

## Script Overview Gate

- Can the story be understood quickly?
- Does every beat have a function?
- Is the ending strong enough?
- Are production risks visible before writing the full script?

## Full Script Gate

- Does the first beat earn attention?
- Is dialogue playable?
- Are visuals specific?
- Is the pacing appropriate to the intended duration?
- Is there a clear turn or escalation?
- Does the ending land without over-explaining?

## Shot Breakdown Gate

- Can a director, editor, or production tool understand what each shot must accomplish?
- Are shot size, camera, action, performance, sound, and edit logic clear?
- Are continuity and coverage concerns documented?
- Is it still a directing document, not a prompt dump?
