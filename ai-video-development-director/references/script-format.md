# Script Format Guide

Use this guide when writing or revising a complete script.

## Script Development Rules

- The script must be shootable.
- The opening must create a reason to continue.
- Each beat must change something: information, emotion, relationship, pressure, or expectation.
- Dialogue should sound performable, not like a summary.
- Visuals should do as much work as possible.
- Sound and silence should be planned, not added as decoration.
- Avoid explaining what the viewer can see.

## Revision Passes

When revising a script, check in this order:

1. Hook clarity.
2. Story logic.
3. Character motivation.
4. Beat rhythm.
5. Dialogue sharpness.
6. Visual specificity.
7. Sound design.
8. Ending payoff.
9. Production feasibility.

## Common Problems

- The concept is strong but the opening is too slow.
- The plot is clear but the character desire is weak.
- The message is good but the scene has no action.
- The ending explains instead of landing.
- The reference influence is visible but not transformed.
